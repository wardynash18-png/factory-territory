# Research — A013 Tinroof (account_id: A013)

## Selection note

The seed claims a Canadian 140-employee Series A AI/ML platform company on `tinroof.dev`. That entity does not resolve. The closest real co is **Tin Roof Software** (US-based software services / staff augmentation firm, ~9,756 LinkedIn followers, Georgia, US — well known outsourcing/devshop). Tin Roof Software → seed gap is severe (US, not Canada; agency/dev-services, not AI/ML product; not Series A; outsourcing model).

Researched by: droid, 2026-07-14. Sources cited inline.

## 1. SIGNAL

**Found, but it is a disqualifying signal.** Tin Roof Software is the type of company that *sells* modernization services — meaning Factory as a vendor would directly eat into their billable hours.

- **Public positioning as modernization partner.** *"Welcome to Tin Roof. We provide enterprises with a full range of software development, solutions architecture, devops, and digital transformation…"* (`https://www.linkedin.com/company/tin-roof-software/`, accessed 2026-07-14, ~9,756 followers).
- **Inc.com profile echoes the same.** *"Tin Roof Software: Develops and provides full stack web, mobile development, and Cloud software."* (`https://www.inc.com/profile/tin-roof-software`).
- **Hiring signal — engineering roles.** Built In lists Tin Roof as actively hiring (`https://builtin.com/company/tin-roof-software`) and leadIQ confirms an ongoing recruiting posture (`https://leadiq.com/c/tin-roof-software/5a1d98b523000053008758d3`). This means they ARE hiring engineers — but to staff client projects, not to do internal modernization.
- **No public engineering blog or public GitHub org under the Tin Roof Software name surfaced in this search.** That absence itself is a signal: a services firm doesn't typically publish engineering thought-leadership at volume.

**The single most defensible SIGNAL is: Tin Roof Software sells "digital transformation" as a service. A Droid that automates the enterprise modernization workstream is a direct competitor to their billable engineering capacity.**

Job-posting signal: **PRESENT but ambiguous.** Multiple aggregator listings exist but a single canonical "Senior Backend Engineer" listing on Tin Roof's own domain was not surfaced. Treat with caution.

## 6. COMPLICATION

- **Wrong-entity risk.** The seed says "Canadian 140-employee Series A AI/ML platform," reality is "US agency with ~9.7k LinkedIn followers." Reach-out with seed framing will land wrong.
- **AGENCY-MODEL DISQUALIFIER.** AGENTS.md rule 1 disqualifiers explicitly include *"agency/reseller model."* Tin Roof Software IS an agency / digital transformation reseller. This is the single most important complication — even if the prospects are good people, the ICP says no.
- **Cannibalization risk.** Even if a deal closed, AI-agent-driven modernization could destroy Tin Roof's core revenue, meaning they may actively resist the buying motion.
- **Leadership freshness.** The named executives (Gore, Chandra, Patel) are confirmed on aggregator profiles but I did not verify them all as currently in seat via LinkedIn in this pass — recommend refreshing before any touch.
- **No public engineering signal.** Absence of an engineering blog and GitHub org reduces the diagnostic confidence we have on their stack — pitching a modernization story requires more research before the first call.
- **Decision is ROI-driven, not engineering-driven.** Tools buying at a devshop is decided on bill-rate math, not DX preference. Cold-email framing has to be commercial, not technical.

## Verdict

`DEFER` (effectively `DISQUALIFY` per AGENTS.md rule 1) — Tin Roof Software is a real firm with the right talking points, but its services/agency business model is an explicit ICP disqualifier and any Factory sale would cannibalize their billable baseline. Drop from active outreach; revisit only if they shift to a product model.
