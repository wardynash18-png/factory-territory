The written thesis (read with the scatter above)

San Diego is a firmware-and-defense town; Denver is a telecom-legacy graveyard (Qwest · CenturyLink · Level 3 · DISH heritage) plus mainframe-era payments.

The scatter above shows a problem: by firmographics alone (revenue × employees), the priority 1 and 2 accounts are impossible to separate from the long tail. The real buying signal is who's hiring a human to do Factory's job — which appears in ZERO firmographic filters. The only place that signal surfaces is in this dashboard's Account detail panel: a Sr Director of Transformation req at DaVita (A002), the HCLTech partnership release for Western Union (A004), the Hyderabad GCC launch for LPL (A005), and the Milliman acquisition announcement for Empower (A006).

What this implies about the Forecast tab: the unweighted pipeline counts every account equally. The MEDDPICC-weight shifts that count by giving dollar weight to verified signals (reqs, press releases, named CIOs) and stripping out account-shape that has no published trigger. Two accounts at "Early" band (A002 DaVita, A004 Western Union) carry disproportionate weight; two accounts at "Unqualified" (A001, A003, A006) collapse. The gap between weighted and unweighted is the honest cost of "negative signals I won't repeat here."

What this implies about the contact list: the 5-slot schema (Economic Buyer · Technical Champion · Program Owner · Security/Governance · Wildcard) is the "humans the rep needs to know." Where the seed suggested a name that this research pass could not verify, the slot is "SLOT ONLY" and the LinkedIn URL is replaced with a people-search link. That is the AGENTS.md rule 2 telling: pattern-constructing a LinkedIn URL is worse than admitting the slot is unfilled.

AGENTS.md: every claim VERIFIED (URL) or INFERRED · disqualifying requires a free-text reason · signal age tracked · LinkedIn URLs come from search results only (never pattern-constructed) · 300-char LinkedIn cap is enforced with red above.
